\section{Introduction}
Topic is the most important classification for not just poetry but for all literary genres. It facilitates categorization of documents into different topics or subjects and assists in efficient retrieval. Earlier, documents had to be manually tagged to topic/s for classification, but with the advent of topic modeling, this can be done automatically without any manual work.

\subsection{LDA}
% - - - - - - - -
Latent Dirichlet Allocation or LDA by Blei et al.\cite{blei2003latent} is a statistical topic model where each document is viewed as a mixture of various topics and is extensively used in topic modeling. We used LDA model to classify all PoFo poems into custom sets. We experimented with varying topic sizes (5 to 30), to observe the quality of the sets formed. To check the quality of the sets, we observed the top words in each set and checked whether they demonstrate any thematic or abstract topical schema.

Table \ref{lda-10-topic-top-words} shows the top 10 words of the topics automatically generated by LDA with 10 topics. It can be observed that there is overlap between the topics with some words like man, light, etc. included in more than 1 topic. The topics generated by LDA are not very useful as it is very difficult to get a thematic representation from them.

\subsection{LSI}
% - - - - - - - -
LSI by Deerwester et al.\cite{deerwester1990indexing} is a method of information retrieval by analyzing relationship between documents and their terms and it uses a mathematical technique Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) for dimensionality reduction. We applied this method on our PoFo dataset to evaluate its performance on our topic classification task.

Table \ref{lsi-10-topic-top-words} shows the top 10 words of the topics automatically generated by LSI with 10 topics. There are many instances of overlap between the words and it is difficult to deduce a theme for the topics.
