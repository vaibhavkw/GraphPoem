
Title : January, 1795 <br>
Author : Mary Robinson <br><br>

Pavement slipp'ry, people <mark3>sneezing,</mark3><br>
Lords in ermine, beggars <mark3>freezing;</mark3><br>
Titled gluttons dainties <mark4>carving,</mark4><br>
Genius in a garret <mark3>starving.</mark3><br>
<br>
Lofty mansions, warm and <mark3>spacious;</mark3><br>
Courtiers cringing and <mark3>voracious;</mark3><br>
Misers scarce the wretched <mark4>heeding;</mark4><br>
Gallant soldiers <mark4>fighting</mark4>, <mark3>bleeding.</mark3><br>
<br>
Wives who laugh at passive <mark3>spouses;</mark3><br>
<mark4>Theatres</mark4>, and <mark3>meeting-houses;</mark3><br>
Balls, where simp'ring misses <mark3>languish;</mark3><br>
Hospitals, and groans of <mark3>anguish.</mark3><br>
<br>
Arts and sciences bewailing; <br>
Commerce <mark4>drooping</mark4>, credit <mark4>failing;</mark4><br>
Placemen mocking subjects <mark3>loyal;</mark3><br>
Separations, weddings <mark3>royal.</mark3><br>
<br>
Authors who can't earn a <mark3>dinner;</mark3><br>
Many a subtle rogue a <mark3>winner;</mark3><br>
Fugitives for shelter <mark4>seeking;</mark4><br>
Misers <mark4>hoarding</mark4>, tradesmen <mark4>breaking.</mark4><br>
<br>
Taste and talents quite <mark3>deserted;</mark3><br>
All the laws of truth <mark3>perverted;</mark3><br>
Arrogance o'er merit <mark4>soaring;</mark4><br>
Merit silently <mark3>deploring.</mark3><br>
<br>
Ladies gambling night and <mark4>morning;</mark4><br>
Fools the works of genius scorning; <br>
Ancient dames for girls <mark3>mistaken,</mark3><br>
Youthful damsels quite <mark3>forsaken.</mark3><br>
<br>
Some in luxury <mark4>delighting;</mark4><br>
More in talking than in <mark3>fighting;</mark3><br>
Lovers old, and beaux decrepid; <br>
Lordlings empty and <mark4>insipid.</mark4><br>
<br>
Poets, <mark4>painters</mark4>, and <mark4>musicians;</mark4><br>
Lawyers, <mark4>doctors</mark4>, <mark3>politicians:</mark3><br>
Pamphlets, <mark4>newspapers</mark4>, and <mark4>odes,</mark4><br>
Seeking fame by diff'rent <mark3>roads.</mark3><br>
<br>
Gallant souls with empty <mark4>purses;</mark4><br>
Gen'rals only fit for <mark3>nurses;</mark3><br>
School-boys, smit with martial <mark4>spirit,</mark4><br>
Taking place of vet'ran <mark4>merit.</mark4><br>
<br>
Honest men who can't get <mark4>places,</mark4><br>
Knaves who shew unblushing <mark3>faces;</mark3><br>
Ruin hasten'd, peace <mark4>retarded;</mark4><br>
Candor spurn'd, and art <mark4>rewarded.</mark4><br>
