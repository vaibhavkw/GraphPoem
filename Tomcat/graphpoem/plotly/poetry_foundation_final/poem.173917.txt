
Title : The Tide Rises, the Tide Falls <br>
Author : Henry Wadsworth Longfellow <br><br>

The tide <mark4>rises</mark4>, the tide <mark4>falls,</mark4><br>
The twilight darkens, the curlew <mark3>calls;</mark3><br>
Along the sea-sands damp and <mark3>brown</mark3><br>
The traveller hastens toward the <mark3>town,</mark3><br>
And the tide <mark4>rises</mark4>, the tide <mark1>falls.</mark1><br>
<br>
Darkness settles on roofs and <mark3>walls,</mark3><br>
But the sea, the sea in the darkness <mark1>calls;</mark1><br>
The little waves, with their soft, white <mark4>hands,</mark4><br>
Efface the footprints in the <mark3>sands,</mark3><br>
And the tide <mark4>rises</mark4>, the tide <mark1>falls.</mark1><br>
<br>
The morning breaks; the steeds in their <mark3>stalls</mark3><br>
Stamp and neigh, as the hostler <mark1>calls;</mark1><br>
The day returns, but <mark4>nevermore</mark4><br>
Returns the traveller to the <mark4>shore,</mark4><br>
And the tide <mark4>rises</mark4>, the tide <mark1>falls.</mark1><br>
