
Title : In Memoriam A. H. H. OBIIT MDCCCXXXIII: 83 <br>
Author : Alfred, Lord Tennyson <br><br>

Dip down upon the northern <mark3>shore</mark3><br>
O sweet new-year delaying <mark3>long;</mark3><br>
Thou doest expectant nature <mark3>wrong;</mark3><br>
Delaying long, delay no <mark3>more.</mark3><br>
<br>
What stays thee from the clouded <mark4>noons,</mark4><br>
         Thy sweetness from its proper place? <br>
Can trouble live with April <mark4>days,</mark4><br>
Or sadness in the summer <mark4>moons?</mark4><br>
<br>
Bring orchis, bring the foxglove <mark4>spire,</mark4><br>
The little speed well's darling <mark3>blue,</mark3><br>
Deep tulips dash'd with fiery <mark3>dew,</mark3><br>
Laburnums, dropping-wells of <mark3>fire.</mark3><br>
<br>
O thou new-year, delaying <mark1>long,</mark1><br>
Delayest the sorrow in my <mark3>blood,</mark3><br>
That longs to burst a frozen <mark3>bud</mark3><br>
And flood a fresher throat with <mark3>song.</mark3><br>
