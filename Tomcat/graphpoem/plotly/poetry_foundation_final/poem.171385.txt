
Title : Poem (At night Chinamen jump) <br>
Author : Frank O'Hara <br><br>

At night Chinamen <mark3>jump</mark3><br>
on Asia with a <mark3>thump</mark3><br>
<br>
while in our willful <mark3>way</mark3><br>
we, in secret, <mark3>play</mark3><br>
<br>
affectionate games and <mark3>bruise</mark3><br>
our knees like China's <mark3>shoes.</mark3><br>
<br>
The birds push apples <mark3>through</mark3><br>
grass the moon turns <mark3>blue,</mark3><br>
<br>
these apples roll <mark3>beneath</mark3><br>
our buttocks like a <mark3>heath</mark3><br>
<br>
full of Chinese <mark4>thrushes</mark4><br>
flushed from China's <mark4>bushes.</mark4><br>
<br>
As we love at <mark3>night</mark3><br>
birds sing out of <mark3>sight,</mark3><br>
<br>
Chinese rhythms <mark4>beat</mark4><br>
through us in our <mark3>heat,</mark3><br>
<br>
the apples and the <mark4>birds</mark4><br>
move us like soft <mark3>words,</mark3><br>
<br>
we couple in the <mark3>grace</mark3><br>
of that mysterious <mark3>race.</mark3><br>
