
Title : Scots Wha Hae <br>
Author : Robert Burns <br><br>

Scots, wha hae wi' Wallace <mark3>bled,</mark3><br>
Scots, wham Bruce has aften <mark3>led;</mark3><br>
Welcome to your gory <mark3>bed,</mark3><br>
Or to <mark4>victory!</mark4><br>
<br>
Now's the day, and now's the <mark3>hour;</mark3><br>
See the front o' battle <mark3>lour;</mark3><br>
See approach proud Edward's <mark3>power--</mark3><br>
Chains and <mark4>slavery!</mark4><br>
<br>
Wha will be a traitor <mark3>knave?</mark3><br>
Wha can fill a coward's <mark3>grave!</mark3><br>
Wha sae base as be a <mark3>slave?</mark3><br>
Let him turn and <mark3>flee!</mark3><br>
<br>
Wha for Scotland's king and <mark3>law</mark3><br>
Freedom's sword will strongly <mark3>draw,</mark3><br>
Freeman stand, or freeman fa', <br>
Let him follow <mark3>me!</mark3><br>
<br>
By oppression's woes and <mark3>pains!</mark3><br>
By your sons in servile <mark3>chains!</mark3><br>
We will drain our dearest <mark3>veins,</mark3><br>
But they shall be <mark3>free!</mark3><br>
<br>
Lay the proud usurpers <mark3>low!</mark3><br>
Tyrants fall in every <mark3>foe!</mark3><br>
Liberty's in every <mark3>blow!--</mark3><br>
         Let us do or die! <br>
