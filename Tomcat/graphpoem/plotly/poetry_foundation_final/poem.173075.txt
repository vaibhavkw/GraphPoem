
Title : A Winter Night <br>
Author : Robert Burns <br><br>

When biting Boreas, fell and doure, <br>
Sharp shivers thro' the leafless <mark3>bow'r;</mark3><br>
When Phoebus gies a short-liv'd <mark3>glow'r,</mark3><br>
Far south the <mark3>lift,</mark3><br>
Dim-dark'ning thro' the flaky <mark3>show'r,</mark3><br>
Or whirling <mark3>drift:</mark3><br>
<br>
Ae night the storm the steeples <mark4>rocked,</mark4><br>
Poor Labour sweet in sleep was <mark3>locked,</mark3><br>
While burns, wi' snawy wreeths upchoked, <br>
Wild-eddying <mark3>swirl,</mark3><br>
Or thro' the mining outlet bocked, <br>
Down headlong <mark3>hurl.</mark3><br>
<br>
List'ning, the doors an' winnocks <mark4>rattle,</mark4><br>
I thought me on the ourie <mark3>cattle,</mark3><br>
Or silly sheep, wha bide this <mark3>brattle</mark3><br>
O' winter <mark4>war,</mark4><br>
And thro' the drift, deep-lairing, <mark5>sprattle,</mark5><br>
Beneath a <mark4>scar.</mark4><br>
<br>
Ilk happing bird, wee, helpless <mark3>thing!</mark3><br>
That, in the merry months o' <mark3>spring,</mark3><br>
Delighted me to hear thee <mark3>sing,</mark3><br>
         What comes o' thee? <br>
Whare wilt thou cow'r thy chittering <mark3>wing</mark3><br>
An' close thy <mark4>e'e?</mark4><br>
<br>
Ev'n you on murd'ring errands <mark4>toil'd,</mark4><br>
Lone from your savage homes exil'd, <br>
The blood-stain'd roost, and sheep-cote <mark3>spoil'd</mark3><br>
My heart <mark4>forgets,</mark4><br>
While pityless the tempest wild <br>
Sore on you <mark4>beats.</mark4><br>
