
Title : To Quilca, a Country House not in Good Repair <br>
Author : Jonathan Swift <br><br>

Let me thy Properties <mark3>explain,</mark3><br>
A rotten <mark4>Cabin</mark4>, dropping <mark3>Rain;</mark3><br>
Chimnies with Scorn rejecting <mark3>Smoak;</mark3><br>
Stools, Tables, Chairs, and Bed-steds <mark3>broke:</mark3><br>
Here Elements have lost their Vses, <br>
Air ripens not, nor Earth <mark4>produces:</mark4><br>
In vain we make poor Sheelah <mark3>toil,</mark3><br>
Fire will not roast, nor Water <mark3>boil.</mark3><br>
Thro' all the Vallies, <mark4>Hills</mark4>, and <mark4>Plains,</mark4><br>
The Goddess Want in Triumph <mark3>reigns;</mark3><br>
And her chief Officers of <mark3>State,</mark3><br>
Sloth, <mark4>Dirt</mark4>, and Theft around her <mark3>wait.</mark3><br>
