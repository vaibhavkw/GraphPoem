
Title : In Memoriam A. H. H. OBIIT MDCCCXXXIII: 99 <br>
Author : Alfred, Lord Tennyson <br><br>

Risest thou thus, dim <mark4>dawn</mark4>, <mark4>again,</mark4><br>
So loud with voices of the <mark3>birds,</mark3><br>
So thick with lowings of the <mark3>herds,</mark3><br>
Day, when I lost the flower of <mark3>men;</mark3><br>
<br>
Who tremblest thro' thy darkling <mark3>red</mark3><br>
On yon swoll'n brook that bubbles <mark3>fast</mark3><br>
By meadows breathing of the <mark3>past,</mark3><br>
And woodlands holy to the <mark3>dead;</mark3><br>
<br>
Who murmurest in the foliaged <mark4>eaves</mark4><br>
A song that slights the coming <mark3>care,</mark3><br>
And Autumn laying here and <mark3>there</mark3><br>
A fiery finger on the <mark3>leaves;</mark3><br>
<br>
Who wakenest with thy balmy <mark4>breath</mark4><br>
To myriads on the genial <mark4>earth,</mark4><br>
Memories of bridal, or of <mark3>birth,</mark3><br>
And unto myriads more, of <mark4>death.</mark4><br>
<br>
O wheresoever those may <mark3>be,</mark3><br>
Betwixt the slumber of the <mark4>poles,</mark4><br>
To-day they count as kindred <mark3>souls;</mark3><br>
They know me not, but mourn with <mark3>me.</mark3><br>
