
Title : In Memoriam A. H. H. OBIIT MDCCCXXXIII: 27 <br>
Author : Alfred, Lord Tennyson <br><br>

I envy not in any <mark4>moods</mark4><br>
The captive void of noble <mark3>rage,</mark3><br>
The linnet born within the <mark3>cage,</mark3><br>
That never knew the summer <mark4>woods:</mark4><br>
<br>
I envy not the beast that <mark3>takes</mark3><br>
His license in the field of <mark3>time,</mark3><br>
Unfetter'd by the sense of <mark3>crime,</mark3><br>
To whom a conscience never <mark3>wakes;</mark3><br>
<br>
Nor, what may count itself as <mark3>blest,</mark3><br>
The heart that never plighted <mark3>troth</mark3><br>
But stagnates in the weeds of <mark3>sloth;</mark3><br>
Nor any want-begotten <mark3>rest.</mark3><br>
<br>
I hold it true, whate'er <mark3>befall;</mark3><br>
I feel it, when I sorrow <mark4>most;</mark4><br>
'Tis better to have loved and <mark4>lost</mark4><br>
Than never to have loved at <mark3>all.</mark3><br>
